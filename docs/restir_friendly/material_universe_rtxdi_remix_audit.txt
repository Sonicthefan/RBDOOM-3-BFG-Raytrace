Material Universe Audit Against RTXDI and RTX Remix
Date: 2026-05-18

Scope
=====

This note compares rbdoom's current RT smoke/path tracing material universe
against:

- RTXDI FullSample material and surface RAB contract.
- RTX Remix material/texture persistence patterns for hostile legacy games.

This is an audit only. No shader or C++ behavior is changed here.


rbdoom Material Path Today
==========================

CPU-side persistent material records:

- neo/renderer/NVRHI/PathTraceMaterialUniverse.h
- neo/renderer/NVRHI/PathTraceMaterialUniverse.cpp

The material universe keys records by Doom material id plus material-name hash.
Each record gets a persistent universeIndex, stable flags, fallback albedo,
texture safety flags, emissive facts, and additive/filter/portal/glass
fallback flags.

CPU-side texture metadata registry:

- neo/renderer/NVRHI/PathTraceTextureRegistry.h
- neo/renderer/NVRHI/PathTraceTextureRegistry.cpp
- neo/renderer/NVRHI/PathTraceMaterialTextureDiscovery.cpp

The texture registry persists material texture metadata by material id. It
tracks image pointers, safe texture handles, texture names, usage/color-format
facts, and a generation counter used by the dynamic material table cache.

Per-frame GPU material table:

- neo/renderer/NVRHI/PathTraceDynamicMaterialState.cpp
- neo/renderer/NVRHI/PathTraceSmokeSceneBuild.cpp

The GPU-facing table can be built from either the legacy path or the material
universe path, controlled by r_pathTracingMaterialUniverseTable. The cache
signature includes texture registry size/generation and several texture CVars,
but not every shader-side material policy CVar.

Shader material supplier:

- neo/shaders/builtin/pathtracing/pathtrace_smoke_rab_material_supplier.hlsli

This reconstructs an RTXDI-shaped RAB_Material from the smoke payload and
PathTraceSmokeMaterial table. It samples diffuse, alpha, normal, specular, and
emissive textures according to TextureInfo flags.

Primary surface history:

- neo/shaders/builtin/pathtracing/PathTracePrimarySurface.hlsli

rbdoom stores its own current/previous primary-surface records. These records
carry world position, geometry normal, shading normal, albedo, roughness,
specular F0, material id, material table index, material flags, surface class,
instance id, primitive id, and emissive texture index.

RAB material similarity:

- neo/shaders/builtin/pathtracing/RtxdiBridge/RAB_Material.hlsli
- neo/shaders/builtin/pathtracing/PathTracePrimarySurface.hlsli

There are two similarity concepts:

1. RAB_Material similarity has an RTXDI-like mode: roughness relative
   difference, specular luminance difference, diffuse luminance difference.
2. PathTracePrimarySurfacesAreSimilar still requires materialId,
   materialIndex, and surfaceClass to match before normal/roughness checks.


RTXDI Reference Shape
=====================

Reference files:

- E:/prog/references/RTXDI-main/Samples/FullSample/Shaders/LightingPasses/RtxdiApplicationBridge/RAB_Material.hlsli
- E:/prog/references/RTXDI-main/Samples/FullSample/Shaders/LightingPasses/RtxdiApplicationBridge/RAB_Surface.hlsli
- E:/prog/references/RTXDI-main/Samples/FullSample/Shaders/RasterizedGBuffer.hlsl
- E:/prog/references/RTXDI-main/Samples/FullSample/Shaders/PrepareLights.hlsl

RTXDI FullSample does not use Doom-style material ids or compact material-table
indices for ReSTIR similarity. It writes current and previous G-buffer material
state, then RAB reads:

- diffuseAlbedo
- specularF0
- roughness
- emissiveColor
- shading normal
- geometric normal
- depth
- motion vector compatible with the depth convention

RAB_AreMaterialsSimilar compares material properties, not authored material
identity. The reference explicitly says to return true if unsure; the material
test is a reuse guard, not an identity/remap system.

RTXDI's emissive light preparation reads material constants and emissive texture
indices from the scene/material system, but ReSTIR material similarity itself is
property based.


RTX Remix Reference Shape
=========================

Reference files:

- E:/prog/references/dxvk-remix-git/src/dxvk/rtx_render/rtx_scene_manager.cpp
- E:/prog/references/dxvk-remix-git/src/dxvk/rtx_render/rtx_scene_manager.h
- E:/prog/references/dxvk-remix-git/src/dxvk/rtx_render/rtx_types.cpp
- E:/prog/references/dxvk-remix-git/src/dxvk/rtx_render/rtx_texture_manager.cpp

RTX Remix is the closer architectural reference for rbdoom because it must deal
with an uncooperative base game. The relevant patterns are:

- surface material cache persists until scene reset;
- texture cache persists and textures are kept alive when referenced;
- per-frame buffer indices are refreshed every frame;
- material and texture categories are derived from original game texture hashes;
- surface material indices are stable across frames;
- GPU material data is uploaded from ordered scene surfaces each frame or when
  scene/material state requires it;
- ray portals and special material types are handled as material/instance
  categories, not as accidental side effects of generic material ids.

Remix separates persistent material identity from frame-local buffer indices.
That separation is the important lesson for rbdoom.


High-Impact Deviations
======================

1. Primary-surface similarity is stricter than RTXDI material similarity.

PathTracePrimarySurfacesAreSimilar rejects when materialId, materialIndex, or
surfaceClass differ. RTXDI's reference RAB_AreMaterialsSimilar only compares
roughness/specular/diffuse properties. In rbdoom, compact materialIndex can
change because of table order, texture probe/table settings, universe-vs-legacy
table mode, or material-table rebuilds. If materialIndex changes while the
surface is otherwise the same, temporal reuse is rejected for a reason RTXDI
does not use.

Impact:

- Can cause cyan/accepted regions to turn into rejection noise during motion or
  after material-table changes.
- Can make portal-adjacent regions look worse if the material table includes
  different static/dynamic/rigid/emissive contributors in different areas.
- Makes a frame-local table index part of a temporal contract.

Recommended slice:

MU-01A: add a debug-only surface-similarity mode that reports whether rejection
comes from materialId, materialIndex, surfaceClass, normal, or roughness. Then
test whether ignoring materialIndex while keeping materialId/surfaceClass
changes the orange/magenta mode-12 failures. Do not change final behavior until
the diagnostic proves the failure mode.


2. Material and texture registries persist, but reset boundaries are unclear.

ClearSmokeMaterialUniverse exists, but current grep only found the function
definition, not a call site. The texture registry has generation tracking but no
obvious clear/reset API in the inspected files. This is not inherently wrong,
but it differs from the Remix pattern where persistent caches have explicit
scene-reset/lifetime ownership and textures are touched/kept alive by live
instances.

Impact:

- The user's report that disabling normal maps poisons mode 12 and turning them
  back on does not recover even after loading another level is consistent with a
  missing reset/invalidation boundary.
- If previous primary surfaces, previous light domains, material universe
  records, texture metadata, and reservoir history survive a semantic material
  mode change, ReSTIR may compare current data against previous data produced
  under different shading-normal/material policy.

Recommended slice:

MU-02A: add a CPU diagnostic "material contract generation" that includes:
r_pathTracingUseNormalMaps, r_pathTracingNormalMapFlipGreen,
r_pathTracingUseSpecularMaps, r_pathTracingUseEmissiveMaps,
r_pathTracingTextureDecode, r_pathTracingMaterialUniverseTable,
SmokeMaterialTextureRegistryGeneration, and material universe stats. Log when
it changes. Do not reset anything in this slice.


3. Shader-side normal/specular/emissive flags are not obviously part of the
material-table cache signature.

TextureInfo.w controls whether normal, specular, and emissive maps participate
in shader material construction. The material table cache signature includes
some texture policy CVars, but the inspected signature does not show
r_pathTracingUseNormalMaps, r_pathTracingUseSpecularMaps, or
r_pathTracingUseEmissiveMaps.

Impact:

- A CVar can change the RAB material/surface produced by the shader without a
  corresponding CPU-side material-table rebuild or ReSTIR history invalidation.
- Normal-map toggling is especially dangerous because RTXDI temporal/spatial
  reuse depends heavily on stable current/previous normals.

Recommended slice:

MU-03A: after MU-02A confirms generation changes, force a ReSTIR/PT history
clear and primary-surface history clear when the material contract generation
changes. Keep it broad and explicit for testing. If it fixes the normal-map
poisoning, narrow the reset scope later.


4. Emissive identity/remap depends on material universe and texture indices.

BuildSmokeEmissiveLightRemap compatibility requires matching identity hash,
materialId, universeMaterialIndex, and emissiveTextureIndex. Emissive triangle
identity also includes materialIndex. This is stricter than Remix's persistent
material/texture hash model and more fragile than RTXDI's material-property
reuse guard.

Impact:

- If materialIndex or emissiveTextureIndex changes due to table ordering or
  texture table rebuild, previous emissive lights fail previous-to-current
  translation.
- This directly matches the mode-12 diagnostic where analytic lights disabled
  produced stationary cyan but motion/portal failures showed emissive
  previous-to-current failures.

Recommended slice:

MU-04A: dump a small sample of emissive remap incompatible records with current
and previous materialId, materialIndex, universeMaterialIndex,
emissiveTextureIndex, material name, emissive texture name, identity hash, and
surface class. This should be one-shot/low-frequency only.


5. rbdoom's material model is still simplified compared with RTXDI/RTRT
shading.

rbdoom's RAB BRDF currently routes mostly through a diffuse-only opaque path,
with optional fake-PBR specular values used for roughness/F0. RTXDI FullSample
uses a clearer GGX/Lambert material model, and RTX Remix has richer opaque
surface material interaction.

Impact:

- This is likely not the immediate strobing bug.
- It can affect target-function magnitude, reservoir weights, and perceived
  brightness, but it should not by itself cause previous-to-current remap
  failures.

Recommended slice:

Do not rewrite BRDF math until the identity/reset/remap issues above are
understood. Keep this as a later conformance task.


Most Likely Link To Current Bug
===============================

The material universe is unlikely to be the only cause of the portal-correlated
orange/magenta ReSTIR debug failures. The current strongest geometry/light
finding remains that emissive remap can be built from a different current
emissive list than the list later uploaded/sampled.

However, the material universe can amplify or preserve the failure:

- materialIndex is frame-local but participates in surface similarity and
  emissive identity;
- universeMaterialIndex and emissiveTextureIndex participate in emissive remap
  compatibility;
- shader-side material/normal policy can change without an obvious ReSTIR
  history reset;
- persistent material/texture registries have no clearly audited scene-reset
  boundary.

The normal-map toggle poisoning behavior is the clearest sign that material
policy changes are not invalidating every history domain that depends on them.


Recommended Order
=================

1. Finish the known emissive remap ordering fix first:

   Build the emissive remap from the same post-merge current/previous emissive
   lists that BuildPathTraceUnifiedLights uploads.

2. Add MU-01A diagnostics:

   Split surface-similarity rejection into materialId, materialIndex,
   surfaceClass, normal, and roughness causes.

3. Add MU-02A diagnostics:

   Track a material contract generation that includes material/normal/specular/
   emissive policy CVars and persistent registry generations.

4. Add MU-03A reset test:

   On material contract generation change, clear ReSTIR/PT reservoirs,
   primary-surface history, previous unified light domains, and any previous
   emissive/analytic remap inputs. This is a proof slice, not a final policy.

5. Add MU-04A remap incompatibility dump:

   If emissive previous-to-current failures remain, dump exact current/previous
   material identity fields for a few incompatible records.


Do Not Do Yet
=============

- Do not rewrite BRDF/material math as a first response.
- Do not remove Doom material flags or material ids globally.
- Do not copy/paste RTXDI or RTX Remix source.
- Do not hide the failure with looser reuse gates until the diagnostic proves
  which identity field is causing rejection.
- Do not treat the material universe as independent from light and geometry
  universes; emissive identity crosses all three.
