COPLANAR NORMAL OFFSET FOR DETAIL-DECAL CARDS
=============================================
Authored: 2026-06-10. The offset that makes coplanar accumulation reliable and
separates stacked decals. Mirrors RTX Remix's per-decal offset
(rtx_instance_manager.cpp:1942; options rtx_options.h:231-259), adapted to this
fork's static, view-independent world BVH.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. THE TWO JOBS THE OFFSET DOES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  J1. Make decal_t < base_t reliably. Any-hit only fires for candidates CLOSER than
      the current committed closest. Coplanar decal and wall are within float
      epsilon, so without an offset whether the decal's any-hit fires depends on
      undefined traversal order — accumulation becomes a coin flip. Lifting the
      decal a hair toward the room guarantees its any-hit fires before the wall
      commits.
  J2. Separate STACKED decals from each other. Two decals on the same wall spot are
      also coplanar with EACH OTHER. An INCREMENTING offset (decal k pushed further
      than decal k-1) keeps their draw/blend order geometrically consistent and
      stops them fighting. This is why the offset is an INDEX, not a fixed epsilon.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. DIRECTION — OUTWARD FACE NORMAL, NEVER CAMERA-WARD
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The static world BVH (instances 0/1) is built ONCE and is VIEW-INDEPENDENT
(BuildRigidLocalSmokeVertex / m_staticVertexCache; 01.E). You CANNOT lift "toward
the viewer" without making the BVH view-dependent and breaking temporal stability.
Lift along the decal triangle's OUTWARD GEOMETRIC FACE NORMAL — which points into
the room where the camera will be anyway. Use the FACE normal (cross of two edges),
NOT smoothed vertex normals: a decal that wraps a corner would splay/tear if pushed
along divergent vertex normals. Push all three verts of the card by the SAME vector
so the card stays planar.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. MAGNITUDE — WORLD UNITS, INCREMENTING INDEX, CAPPED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
offset = faceNormal * (offsetIndex * r_pathTracingDecalOffsetStep)
  - r_pathTracingDecalOffsetStep: world-space step (D3 units ~ inches). START
    ~0.1-0.25. Too small -> distant z-fight returns (TLAS float precision); too
    large -> visible PEELING / floating at grazing angles + poke-through of nearby
    perpendicular walls. Single tuned cvar. (A prior implementation attempt found a
    conservative ~0.05 was workable as the LOW end — treat as a starting point to
    tune up from, not a validated default; confirm against V3 before trusting it.)
  - offsetIndex: a monotonically-incrementing counter (Remix's "global decal offset
    index"). Increment per detail-decal card (or per draw-call batch — see sec.4).
    Cap at r_pathTracingDecalMaxOffsetIndex (Remix: decals.maxOffsetIndex; a prior
    attempt started this at 4); beyond the cap, reuse the max (accept rare mis-stack
    rather than unbounded lift).
  - Consider distance-scaling ONLY if a fixed step shows distant z-fight after
    tuning; a constant world step is the v1 default.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. PER-CARD vs PER-BATCH (Remix singleOffsetDecalTextures)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Remix offers two modes (rtx_options.h:245-258):
  - PER-CARD (default here): each card gets its own incrementing index. Correct for
    stacked decals; grows the index fastest.
  - PER-BATCH (singleOffsetDecalTextures): one shared offset for all cards in a
    draw-call batch that are known NOT to inter-overlap. Grows the index slower,
    stabilizes batched dynamic decals.
v1: PER-CARD. Expose a per-batch cvar only if index growth hits the cap in real
levels. Also mirror nonOffsetDecalTextures intent: if any source geometry is ALREADY
offset from the base, skip our offset (rare for D3; a cvar/skip-flag, not v1 work).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. WHERE IT LANDS (the seam)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Per-triangle/per-surface STATIC capture in PathTraceGeometryUniverse.cpp (NOT the
per-vertex BuildRigidLocalSmokeVertex:738). At the surface level you have all three
positions + the material together, so you can:
  (a) classify isDetailDecal (DECAL-01 cached predicate),
  (b) compute the face normal from the triangle,
  (c) push the three baked positions by offset before they enter m_staticVertexCache.
The offset is baked into the captured vertex positions (zero trace-time cost), not
applied at trace time. Because the decal is never the SHADED surface (it any-hit
accumulates; the wall is the closest hit), the lifted position never reaches the
G-buffer — depth/normal/position all come from the base. The offset is invisible
except as the thing that makes accumulation deterministic.

CORRECTNESS GUARD (learned from a prior attempt — keep it): the offset lifts the
decal toward the room, which means a decal's any-hit can fire on rays that are NOT
actually hitting its receiver — the ray may be skimming past and the lifted card
sits in its path, or the decal belongs to a wall on the far side of a thin door.
So the shader MUST validate each accumulated decal against the committed base hit
before compositing: keep the decal only if its hit lies within the offset envelope
of the base surface (i.e. the decal is within ~maxOffsetIndex*step of the base
along the base normal). CRITICAL: measure this in NORMAL space (distance along the
base/decal face normal), NOT as raw ray-T distance — at grazing angles a small
normal offset becomes a large along-ray T, so a ray-T cutoff produces circular
halos and wrongly drops valid grazing decals. Without this guard you get decals
bleeding through doors/thin walls and onto unrelated nearby cards.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. PITFALLS / DONE-CRITERIA
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - Peeling sliver at grazing angles: minimized by small step + applying only to the
    tagged detail-decal subset (never to large surfaces).
  - Poke-through of perpendicular neighbors: cap the step; verify in a corner-dense
    level (alphalabs/mars_city).
  DONE: with the offset on, a detail decal accumulates EVERY frame held-still (no
  flicker), stacked decals (e.g. grime over a panel seam) keep a stable order, and
  no visible floating at normal viewing distances.
