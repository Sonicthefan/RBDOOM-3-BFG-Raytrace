*** BINDING ADDENDUM — ARCHITECTURE IS DECIDED. This plan implements ONE chosen
*** architecture: BLEND-THROUGH IN THE BVH via any-hit accumulation. The decal
*** card stays as geometry in the existing world BLAS; on a ray hit the decal is
*** accumulated into the payload and IgnoreHit()'d, the base wall stays the
*** committed closest hit, and the decal is composited onto the base material
*** deterministically at surface-build time. The alternative architectures
*** (clustered/separate-decal-TLAS box projection, or load-time bake) were
*** evaluated and REJECTED for this fork — do not reintroduce them. Rationale and
*** the rejected options are in 02 and 09.
***
*** HARD ENGINE CONSTRAINT: PathTraceSmokeResources.cpp:979 sets
*** maxRecursionDepth = 1. The closest-hit shader CANNOT TraceRay. Every design
*** choice here is shaped by that: accumulation happens in ANY-HIT (which runs
*** before commit and needs no recursion), never via a re-trace from closest-hit.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
0. ONE-LINE GOAL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Make idTech4 STATIC GEOMETRY DETAIL DECALS (the coplanar cards that drive real
level texture detail — grime, trim, panels, signage, scorch) render correctly in
the path tracer by COMPOSITING them onto the surface they sit on, with correct
over / modulate / additive blending across a full material layer, instead of the
current stochastic any-hit rejection that can only ever show decal-OR-base and
cannot express modulate.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. WHY NOW — WHAT IS BROKEN TODAY (audited 2026-06-10)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Today ALL cards (smoke, additive glows, alpha cards, AND geometry detail decals)
go through ONE mechanism: stochastic any-hit rejection in
pathtrace_primary_surface_producer.rt.hlsl (SmokeAlphaRejectsHit:1241, the
SmokeAdditiveDecalRejectsHit:1109 / SmokeFilterDecalRejectsHit:1132 helpers, and
AnyHit:1329). Per ray the card either "wins" (you shade the card's own albedo) or
"loses" (the ray passes through to whatever is behind). Two concrete failures for
geometry detail decals:

  GAP-1  Modulate/filter math is unrepresentable. A `blend filter` grime/trim
         decal must compute decal*base (darken/tint the wall underneath).
         Stochastic rejection returns EITHER decal OR base, never the product, so
         SmokeFilterDecalRejectsHit (1132) approximates with a coverage
         probability — a near-black grime decal becomes random black SPECKLE
         instead of a smoothly darkened wall.

  GAP-2  Coplanar z-fight noise. Detail decals are coplanar with the wall (raster
         used MF_POLYGONOFFSET; the BVH has no polygon offset). Which triangle the
         ray "hits first" is precision-dependent, so even before the stochastic
         dice-roll the result is unstable frame to frame.

The classifier already TAGS these correctly (projectedDecal,
PathTraceMaterialClassifier.cpp:429; polygonOffsetDecal/MF_POLYGONOFFSET,
PathTraceDoomMaterialClassifier.cpp:188) — the wrong thing is the SHADING
MECHANISM, not the classification. This plan replaces the mechanism for the
detail-decal subset only.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. SCOPE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
IN SCOPE (this plan):
  - A classifier predicate that separates GEOMETRY DETAIL DECAL (deterministic
    composite) from EFFECT CARD (keep stochastic). Reuses existing tags.
  - A draw-order decal sort key captured per detail-decal surface (deterministic
    blend ordering — coplanar depth is unreliable, draw order is what raster used).
  - An incrementing normal-offset applied to detail-decal card geometry at static
    capture, to make coplanar accumulation reliable and to separate STACKED decals.
  - Payload decal-bin fields + any-hit accumulation of up to N (3-4) decals.
  - Deterministic composite (over / modulate / additive) onto the base material at
    surface-build time, across albedo + emissive (v1), roughness/metallic/F0 (v1.5).
  - Debug view + validation gate.
  - DYNAMIC detail decals (the cinematic glow-card: appears, animates, is removed)
    as a defined LATER phase (07 / DECAL-DYN-x), bridging to the material_classifer
    DYN-x arc. The detail-decal vs effect-card decision is GEOMETRIC, not gated on
    staticness, so dynamic decals still composite correctly.

NOT IN SCOPE (explicitly deferred — do not chase here):
  - VOLUMETRICS / smoke / additive effect sprites. They stay on the EXISTING
    stochastic any-hit path unchanged; the short-term bar for them is only
    "roughly on par with the old raster cards." See 02 sec.6. Do NOT route effect
    cards through the deterministic composite.
  - Box-projection decals onto arbitrary/curved receivers (the RTG II Ch.27 core
    model). D3 static detail decals are authored as flat cards on flat receivers;
    the card model is sufficient and the box model is rejected (09 sec.1).
  - EXACT decal normal-map projection (tangent-frame reconciliation). v2 only; an
    APPROXIMATE base-TBN version is the most we attempt, and only after v1/v1.5.
    Even RTX Remix punts exact normal blending (09 sec.2, resolve.slangh:1398).
  - GENERAL per-instance dynamic fade/flipbook for non-decal materials (that stays
    the material_classifer DYN-x arc). This plan only pulls in the dynamic behavior
    a DETAIL DECAL needs (animated color/alpha, condition toggle, glow-as-light) and
    only in Phase 6 — see 07.
  - Any ReSTIR / NEE-cache / DLSS-RR changes.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. KNOWN PROJECT CONSTRAINTS TO RESPECT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  (a) maxRecursionDepth = 1 (PathTraceSmokeResources.cpp:979). Accumulation in
      any-hit only; no closest-hit re-trace.
  (b) The static world BVH (instances 0/1, where detail decals live — see ClosestHit
      instanceId>=2u route split at pathtrace_primary_surface_producer.rt.hlsl:1365)
      is built ONCE and is VIEW-INDEPENDENT. The geometric normal-offset must be
      along the decal's OUTWARD FACE NORMAL (view-independent), NEVER toward the
      camera, or BVH stability / temporal churn breaks. See 04.
  (c) Classification is cached per materialId and temporally stable. The
      detail-decal predicate is a pure read of idMaterial; it lives in the cached
      record (material_classifer 02 STABILITY), adds nothing per-frame.
  (d) Shader-rebuild gotcha: editing a .hlsli may not trigger a rebuild — delete
      the affected .rt.bin first. See 05 BUILD/DEPLOY and the shader-rebuild-gotcha
      memory.
